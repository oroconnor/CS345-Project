2.   Entry Verification:

     Data are analyzed for outliers and anomalies using the outlier macro for 
Excel 5.0 developed by the CDMO.  These macros discover outlier values which 
fall outside the range that the instrument is designed to measure (see section 
II.8 of this document). After outliers and anomalous data are identified, 
alldata are graphed in Excel using the CDMO graphing macro to help identify 
anomalous data that fall within the range of the probes and also anomalous 
trends. Missing data are identified using the CDMO Excel Date, Hour Count macro.  
All columns are formatted using the CDMO Excel Column Reformat macro.  All data 
was FTPed to the CDMO via the CDMO dial-up access.  All raw data files are 
archived at the CDMO and on 3.5" disks. Questionable data are either deleted or 
identified in section II.10.  All missing data and deleted data will be 
identified in section II.11.

Person responsible for data verification:    Research
Coordinator and Research Assistant, Stephanie Matteson.

3.   Research Objectives:

     Measurements are taken every 30 minutes over a two to four week period from 
April 1 through the end of December of each year  at three locations within the 
Tivoli Bays component of the Hudson River NERR.  One datalogger monitors 
tributary water (Saw Kill, SK) entering the bay and the others monitor the 
Hudson River water flooding and ebbing to and from the bay (TS and TN).  The 
objective of this study is to monitor the quality of the surface water entering 
(via stream flow and tidal exchange) and leaving (via tidal exchange) Tivoli 
South Bay and Tivoli North Bay.  This will help us understand the relative 
importance on each source of water in Tivoli Bay.  The Reserve staff have 
identified urban and residential land use practices as having a major influence 
on the water chemistry of the Saw Kill.  Since residential coverage continues to 
increase, we hope that the intensive monitoring of the surface waters in this 
watershed will identify trends caused by this rapid development.  

4.   Research Methods:  

     Two YSI6000 dataloggers have been placed at the Tivoli Bays component site 
of the Reserve and one YSI6600 has been placed in Tivoli South Bay.  The SK and 
TS instruments are deployed 0.5 meters off the bottom in vertically mounted 
perforated PVC tubes.  The TN instrument is deployed in a rectangular 
24"x13"x13" cage with 1/2 inch plastic coated mesh weighed down with 4 bricks 
zip tied to the cage.  The sonde is placed horizontally in the cage strapped in 
with bungee cord and rests .25 meters from the bottom of the cage. It is 
deployed with a cable attached to the top of the cage and other end anchored to 
the sediment.  Instruments are deployed for a two to four week duration from 
April through December each year.  Data are collected at 30 min. intervals. 
After instruments are retrieved, instrument calibration is checked against 
standards, data are uploaded, and instrument probes are serviced.  Before each 
YSI 6000/6600 is deployed, calibration and maintenance is performed following 
the manufacturer's instructions (YSI Manual addendum 7/94, sections 3,4, and 7 
as well as the YSI 6-series operations manual). Data are uploaded via a cable to 
a laptop computer. Calibration standards are required for pH, turbidity, and 
conductivity; all other parameters are done as described in the manual. Buffer 
solutions for 2 point calibration (pH 7 and 10), conductivity and turbidity 
standards are purchased pre-made from a scientific supply house. The dissolved 
oxygen membranes are replaced before deployment and are allowed to sit at least 
24 hrs prior to deployment. 
	Please note that during the QA/QC review of the depth data for Tivoli 
South Bay (TS), we discovered a 3.0 meter increase in the depth from July 
through December 1999 corresponding with deployment of the YSI 6600 datasonde.  
This was caused by using an unvented calibration cable on the YSI 6600 in the 
lab during the calibration procedures.  The use of this cable caused an increase 
in pressure and offset the zero calibration of the depth probe by an average of 
+2.9834 meters.  Therefore, the temporal cycle of the tidal data from July 1 
till the end of December, 1999 is correct but the absolute depth will be 
approximately 2.98 meters too deep.  

5.   Site Location and Character:

     The Hudson River National Estuarine Research Reserve (HUDNERR) is a 
multicomponent site composed of approximately 5,000 acres of tidal wetland, open 
water, and adjacent upland buffer habitat.  The four component sites of the 
Reserve are located on the Hudson River in New York State by river miles 
(RM)proceeding north from the southern tip of Manhattan.  These include: 
Stockport Flats (RM 124)(42o02'30"N 73o46'00"W), Columbia County; Tivoli Bays 
(RM 98)(42o02'15"N 73o55'10"W), Dutchess County; and Iona Island (RM 
45)(41o18'15"N 73o58'45"W) and Piermont Marsh (RM 24)(41o02'30"N 73o54'15"W), 
Rockland County.  These four components are located along 100 river miles of the 
Hudson and are representative of the diverse plant and animal communities that 
occupy the salinity gradient within the Hudson estuary.  Development within the 
watersheds of Hudson River NERR component sites ranges from predominantly 
urban/suburban to forested/agricultural. 
     The two YSI6000 and one YSI6600 dataloggers are placed at Tivoli Bays 
component site. The Saw Kill (SK) (latitude 42 01’ 01.543” N, longitude 73 54’ 
53.589” W) drains a 26.6 square mile (6886.5 hectares) watershed that is 
characterized as a highly mixed land use watershed.  These are freshwater sites 
(0.0 ppt salinity). Water depth at ranges from 0.0 to 2.0m at TS and 1.0 to 2.0 
at SK.  Bottom type at TS is rocky and at SK is soft silt/clay.  The dominant 
land uses within this watershed include: forested (51.1%), agriculture (25.8%, 
and urban (16.5%).  Tivoli South Bay (TS) (latitude 42 01’ 37.336” N, longitude 
73 55’ 33.445” W), the receiving body for the Saw Kill, is a tidal freshwater 
wetland with a tidal range of 3.9 ft. (1.19 m). Tivoli North Bay (TN) (latitude 
42 02’ 11.56464” N, longitude 73 55’ 31.16645” W) is a freshwater tidal marsh 
dominated by Typha angustifolia.  The primary watershed to TN is 23 square miles 
in size and is dominated by agricultural lands.  The Stony Creek is the 
principle tributary to Tivoli North Bay.

6.   Data collection period:

     YSI6000 datalogger sampling at the Saw Kill (SK) and Tivoli South Bay (TS) 
began during May, 1995.  Sampling at Tivoli North Bay (TN) began during July, 
1996, however ceased in 1997-1998 because at low tide the instrument was 
exposed.  In 1999 an alternate site was established and sampling began in July 
of 1999 .  All sampling is ongoing, however, we cannot sample from approximately 
mid December through March 31 due to winter storms and ice on the Hudson 
Riverand tributaries.  The YSI6600 datalogger was deployed at the 
Tivoli South Bay site replacing the YSI6000 starting on July 1, 1999 @ 15:30:00.

7.   Associated researchers and projects:

     Hudson River NERR Water Quality Monitoring: Physical and chemical 
constituents of the tributary waters discharging into HUDNERR marshes and marsh 
waters during ebb tides are measured monthly and during storm events.  
Concentrations of nitrate, phosphate, sulphate, dissolved oxygen (DO), and 
chloride are measured, as well as, seston, alkalinity, pH, temperature, 
salinity, and conductivity.  
     Saw Kill Water Quality Monitoring:  The surface water draining from five 
subcatchments representing five dominant land use practices within the Saw Kill 
have been monitored during several storm events.  These five land use practices 
include: row crop agriculture, orchard, residential, solid waste landfill, and
forested.  All of the above constituents have been monitored during this 
project.
     Associated researchers with the work at Tivoli Bays include:  scientists 
from the Institute of Ecosystem Studies, Millbrook, NY; Yale School of Forestry 
and Environmental Studies, New Haven, CT; and Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, 
Troy, NY.

12. Other Remarks

During the QA/QC review of the depth data for Tivoli South Bay, we discovered a 
3.0 meter increase in the depth from July through December 1999 corresponding 
with deployment of the YSI6600 datasonde.  This was caused by using an unvented 
calibration cable on the YSI6600 in the lab during the calibration procedures.  
The use of this cable caused an increase in the pressure and offset the zero 
calibration of the depth probe by an average of +2.9834 meters.  Therefore, the 
temporal cycle of the tidal data from July 1 till the end of December 1999 is 
correct but the absolute depth will be approximately 2.98 meters too deep.
